What is the Giving Pledge?

The Giving Pledge is an effort to help address the world’s most pressing problems by inviting the world’s engineers and technologists to commit to giving some portion of their wealth to philanthropy or charitable causes that they believe will do the most good for the world.
Who can pledge?

The Giving Pledge is specifically focused on engineers and individuals with backgrounds in technology, though anyone is allowed to pledge.

The idea takes its inspiration from practices in science using evidence, such as randomized controlled trials, to reason about the effectiveness of different interventions, and from the recognition of extreme inequalities in the world that technology has done not been able to resolve alone.

Given the rising prominence of technology, we felt the prime beneficiaries of this change - engineers or other technologists - could do a lot of good to help those most left behind, and make the world a much better place.
Why a pledge?

We hope that a group coming forward to be explicit about their intentions to give a portion of their wealth to effective causes will help:

  • Inspire conversations, discussions, and action, not just about how much but also for what purposes / to what end and
  • Bring together those committed to this kind of giving to exchange knowledge on how to do this in the best possible way.

We live in an exciting time for philanthropy where innovative approaches and advances in technology have redefined what’s possible. Recent changes in the quality of life for the world's poorest are proving every day how a single individual, regardless of wealth, can make a lasting impact on the lives of others.
Why go public with a pledge?

The goal is to talk about giving in an open way and create an atmosphere that can draw more people into philanthropy.
How does the Giving Pledge work?

Each individual who chooses to pledge will make this statement publicly, along with a statement explaining their decision to pledge.

At an annual event, those who take the pledge will come together to share ideas and learn from each other.

Throughout the year, there are opportunities for conversations that go deeper on the specific topics of interest to the group.
What will people pledge to?

The pledge does not involve pooling money or supporting a particular set of causes or organizations. The pledge asks only that the individual give a portion of their wealth to philanthropic causes or charitable organizations that they believe will do the most good.

Though Givewell is encouraged as a starting point for evidence about effective charity work, the pledge encourages signatories to do their own research to find ways to give that most benefit society.
Will the Giving Pledge grant money to organizations?

Not directly. Each person who pledges makes an individual decision about which particular causes or organizations they wish to support.
How much should people give?

Each family will decide that for themselves. The pledge asks for a commitment to give to causes that an individual believes will do the most good. Many come to different conclusions on this question. Givewell provides a good starting point for evidence about the effectiveness of various forms of charity work
Once someone pledges, how will you make sure they follow through?

The pledge is a moral commitment to give, not a legal contract.
How did the idea come about?

The idea of the Giving Pledge came from the ideas and input generated in many great conversations between friends, inspired by ideas in the essay "Famine, Affluence, and Morality", and in recent press about the effect of technology on inequality more broadly.
How long will the pledge last?

Our hope is that the effort will continue until many of the most severe inequalities in health, shelter, or nutrition are alleviated.
